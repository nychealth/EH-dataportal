Climate and Health in East Flatbush - Flatbush

Extreme heat, coastal storms, flooding and episodes of elevated ozone are climate-related hazards that may increase with climate change and have important public health impacts in New York City. Extreme weather can cause power outages, which also threaten public health. This report provides neighborhood indicators of climate-related hazards, vulnerability and health impacts.

Coastal flood risk

Land percentage in hurricane evacuation zone, 2013

In East Flatbush - Flatbush, land percentage in hurricane evacuation zone is Middle compared to the NYC average.

Hurricane Evacuation Zones are determined by New York City Emergency Management and indicate varying threat levels of coastal flooding from storm surge. Evacuation orders may be given during hurricanes or coastal flooding events. There are six zones, ranked by storm surge risk. Zone 1 is the highest risk for flooding. Residents in these zones may receive evacuation orders during hurricanes or tropical storms.

What is the City doing?
  • Plants trees, funds Cool Roof projects, and supports heat awareness trainings and more to address extreme heat.
  • Set a goal to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050 using the OneNYC strategic plan.
  • Helps low-income households buy and install air conditioners.
  • Operates the largest electric municipal fleet in the nation.
  • Supports energy-efficiency changes in municipal and community buildings.
What can you do?
  • Be a Buddy and check in on neighbors to help them stay cool when its hot.
  • Get info during weather emergencies.
  • Request a street tree to help cool your neighborhood.

Indicator ranking

Each indicator is placed in one of three categories:

  • Better
  • Middle
  • Worse

This shows us how the neighborhood compares to other NYC neighborhoods, if you ranked all of the NYC neighborhoods based on this indicator.

  • Better: in the top 1/3rd of all NYC neighorhoods.
  • Middle: it's close to the NYC median - ranking somewhere in the middle third of all neighborhoods.
  • Worse: in the bottom 1/3rd of NYC neighborhoods.

These are only based on the distribution of data values, not the range. The absolute values of indicators in different categories may not be all that different.
