How to use the story map

This story map can be used to visualize the firsthand experiences of New Yorkers with heat events in their neighborhoods, on their commutes, and during their daily routines. Click on the map markers to view the stories from New Yorkers in that area. The map combines the results of an urban heat island mapping campaign with personal stories about heat. By observing the location of these events in the context of historical and demographic data, the aim of this project is to make visible the relationship between the disproportionate impacts of extreme heat and social inequities. To toggle between [maps / map layers] select the geographical data you’d like to see. You can [select multiple layers at once to see for example daytime surface
