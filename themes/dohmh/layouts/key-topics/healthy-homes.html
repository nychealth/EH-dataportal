Moisture issues

Why it's dangerous

Water leaks and moisture issuess such as leaks, standing water, or moisture thatwon't dry can attract pests and cause mold growth.

Pests and mold can trigger or worsen asthma and allergies, and can create unsanitary conditions in the home.

What you can do

Fix leaks and dry out damp areas, especially in kitchen and bathrooms, by opening a window or running a fan while showering or cooking. Use a dehumidifier to lower humidity levels in your home. Use water and detergent to clean mold on walls or other hard surfaces with small areas of mold.

Report mold, water leaks, and plumbing that needs fixing to building management. If leaks aren't fixed or you think that repair work is done unsafetly, call 311

NYCHA residents should call 718-707-7771 to report a maintenance problem.

If you or your child has asthma and there are pests or mold in your home, your health care provider or asthma case manager can make a referral to the Healthy Neighborhoods Program.

Why it's dangerous
Peeling or damaged paint can contain lead (especially in older buildings), which is poisonous to ingest. This can be risky for children and pets.

What you can do
Report peeling paint to your landlord. If the problem is not fixed or if you think repair work is being done unsafely, call 311 to request a free inspection.

Learn more about lead poisoning prevention
Remind your provider to test your child for lead poisoning at ages 1 and 2 and ask about testing older children if you have peeling paint. Call 311 to find a provider. Visit the NYC Health Department's page on lead poisoning sources and prevention.
