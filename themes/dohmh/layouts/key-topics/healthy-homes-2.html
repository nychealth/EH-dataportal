Pest droppings

Why it's dangerous

Cockroach and mouse droppings are a sign of pests and can worsen allergies and asthma symptoms.

What you can do

To get rid of pests and keep them from coming back, deprive them of food, water, shelter and ways to get around.

Report gaps, cracks or holes and other conditions that can attract pests to building management.

Moisture issues

Why it's dangerous

Water leaks and moisture issues such as leaks, standing water, or moisture that won't dry can attract pests and cause mold growth.

Pests and mold can trigger or worsen asthma and allergies, and can create unsanitary conditions.

What you can do

Keep your home dry by running a fan or opening a window. Use a dehumidifier to lower humidity levels. Use water and detergent to clean small areas of mold on walls or other hard surfaces.

Report, mold, water leaks and plumbing issues to building management.

Open food

Why it's dangerous

Open food packages, pet food left out and garbage cans that don’t close tightly can attract roaches and mice. These pests can make asthma and allergy symptoms worse.

What you can do

Block access to food sources.

Clean up clutter, keep food in sealed containers, don’t leave pet food out, and store trash in a can with a tight-fitting lid. Rinse recyclables before sorting them.

Mold

Why it's dangerous

Stains or visible mold growth on walls may be a sign of a moisture problem.

Mold can cause or worsen allergies and asthma.

What you can do

Use water and soap (or dish detergent) to clean mold on walls or other hard surfaces.

Fix leaks from faucets or pipes.

Report, mold, water leaks and plumbing issues to building management.

Gaps, cracks or holes

Why it's dangerous

Gaps, cracks or holes can let in pests like mice and cockroaches.

Cockroaches and mice can contaminate food, damage homes, and make allergies and asthma worse.

What you can do

To get rid of pests and keep them from coming back, you have to deprive them of food, water, shelter and ways to get around.

Seal pests out. Report gaps, cracks or holes and other conditions that can attract pests to building management.

Peeling paint

Why it's dangerous

Peeling or damaged paint can contain lead, especially in older buildings.

Lead can cause serious health effects. It is especially dangerous for children and pregnant people but can be harmful to anyone.

What you can do

Report peeling paint to your landlord.

Remind your provider to test your child for lead poisoning at ages 1 and 2 and ask about testing older children if you have peeling paint. Call 311 to find a provider.

Dirty shoes kept indoors

Why it's dangerous

Dust and debris from worksites can contain lead.

Lead can cause serious health effects. It is especially dangerous for children and pregnant people but can be harmful to anyone.

What you can do

Remove dirty work shoes before entering your home. Keep your home clean. Wash floors, windowsills, hands and toys often.

Remind your provider to test your child for lead poisoning at ages 1 and 2 and ask about testing older children if you have peeling paint. Call 311 to find a provider.

Using cold water from the sink to make baby formula

Why it's dangerous

Some buildings or homes may have lead pipes and plumbing fixtures.

When water comes in contact with old plumbing and pipes that contain lead, it can get into drinking water. Higher lead levels may be found when the water has not been used for several hours. Also, lead dissolves more easily into hot water.

Lead can cause serious health effects. It is especially dangerous for children and pregnant people but can be harmful to anyone.

What you can do

Use only cold tap water to make baby formula and for drinking and cooking. Run your tap for at least 30 seconds, until the water is noticeably colder.

NYC residents can call 311 to get a free lead test kit for drinking water. You will receive the results within 30 days of submitting the water sample.

Remind your provider to test your child for lead poisoning at ages 1 and 2 and ask about testing older children if you have peeling paint. Call 311 to find a provider.

Cooking with traditional glazed ceramicware

Why it's dangerous

Traditional or handmade ceramicware from around the world can contain lead.

The lead can get into food and drink when these types of products are used to cook or store food and drinks.

Lead can cause serious health effects. It is especially dangerous for children and pregnant people but can be harmful to anyone.

What you can do

Do not use traditional or handmade glazed ceramic ware to prepare, cook, serve, or store food or drinks. These ceramic wares may contain high levels of lead.

You can use these types of traditional ceramic ware for decoration. If you have used these products to prepare, cook, serve or store food, ask your doctor for a blood lead test.

Household cleaners, pesticides under the kitchen sink

Why it's dangerous

Common household products like bleach, ammonia, pest repellents, detergents, and more are poisonous and should be kept somewhere pets and children can’t reach.

What you can do

Keep harmful household products on a high shelf or in locked cabinet that children can’t reach.

Smoke or carbon monoxide detectors that don’t work, need new batteries, or are located too close to the kitchen.

Why it's dangerous

Fire spreads fast and carbon monoxide (CO) is a colorless and odorless gas that can cause illness and sudden death.

70% of fire deaths have occurred in homes where there were no working smoke or CO detectors.

What you can do

Ask your building owner to install a smoke and a CO detector in or near the sleeping areas of your home.

Test your detector regularly by pressing the test button. Replace detectors every 10 years or when the detector signals that it has reached the end of life and needs to be replaced.

Keep detector clean by regularly dusting or vacuuming.

Do not put detectors within five feet of gas burning appliances such as stoves and dryers or near cooking areas.
