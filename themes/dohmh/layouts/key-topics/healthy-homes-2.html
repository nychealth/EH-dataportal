Moisture issues

Why it's dangerous

Water leaks and moisture issuess such as leaks, standing water, or moisture that won't dry can attract pests and cause mold growth.

Pests and mold can trigger or worsen asthma and allergies, and can create unsanitary conditions in the home.

What you can do

Fix leaks and dry out damp areas, especially in kitchen and bathrooms, by opening a window or running a fan while showering or cooking. Use a dehumidifier to lower humidity levels in your home. Use water and detergent to clean mold on walls or other hard surfaces with small areas of mold.

Report mold, water leaks, and plumbing that needs fixing to building management. If leaks aren't fixed or you think that repair work is done unsafetly, call 311

NYCHA residents should call 718-707-7771 to report a maintenance problem.

If you or your child has asthma and there are pests or mold in your home, your health care provider or asthma case manager can make a referral to the Healthy Neighborhoods Program.

Mold

Why it's dangerous

Stains or visible mold growth on walls may be a sign of a moisture problem.

This needs to be fixed to stop mold from growing and causing or worsening allergies and asthma.

What you can do

Use water and soap (or dish detergent) to clean mold on walls or other hard surfaces.

Fix leaks from faucets or pipes.

Report, mold, water leaks and plumbing issues to building management. If the leaks are not being fixed or you think that repair work is being done unsafely, call 311.

Gaps, cracks or holes

Why it's dangerous

Gaps, cracks or holes can let in pests like mice and cockroaches.

Cockroaches and mice can contaminate food, damage homes, and make allergies and asthma worse.

What you can do

To get rid of pests and keep them from coming back, you have to deprive them of food, water, shelter and ways to get around.

Seal pests out. Report gaps, cracks or holes and other conditions that can attract pests to building management.

If the repairs are not being made or you think that repair work is being done unsafely, call 311.

NYCHA residents should call 718-707-7771 to report a maintenance problem.
