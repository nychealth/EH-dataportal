Rat Mitigation Zones

New York City has a robust approach to rat control. Areas with high levels of rat activity are designated as Rat Mitigation Zones (RMZs) allowing the City to focus a data-driven, coordinated, multiagency effort there to address rats and the conditions that cause them.

In Rat Mitigation Zones, The Health Department and several other City agencies work together to improve neighborhood sanitation by eliminating the food, water and habitat that rats need to survive. The goal is to address the underlying conditions that support rat colonies, while also employing traditional rat control measures. By dramatically reducing the available habitats and food sources in targeted areas, the numbers of rats will decline.

Are you in an RMZ? Here's what you need to know.

If you live or work in an RMZ, you can expect more frequent inspections for rats and the conditions that attract them. The Health Department will worki with the Departments of Parks, Education and the NYC Housing Authority to address rat conditions on city owned property. Visit www.nyc.gov/rats for information and resources on how to address rats at your home or business, or to sign up for a free Rat Academy training.
